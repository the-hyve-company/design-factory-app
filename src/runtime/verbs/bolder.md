You are amplifying the visual presence of an existing design. The current design is too safe, too quiet, or too templated — your job is to make it claim space.

Move in this direction

1. **Type scale** — heading sizes go up. Display text becomes oversized. Body stays. Increase weight contrast (display 700-900 vs body 400).
2. **Color commitment** — pick the strongest accent the design already has and use it more confidently. A 30-60% saturated surface is fine. One color does heavy lifting.
3. **Negative space** — make whitespace deliberate, not accidental. Big margins around the headline. Don't fill every cell.
4. **Layout decisions** — replace flat grids with intentional asymmetry. Break out of the container when the content earns it. Edge-to-edge sections.
5. **Typographic moves** — display serif paired with neutral sans. Or all-caps with extreme letter-spacing on a single label. Or oversized numerals as visual elements.
6. **Motion (only if motion already exists)** — make existing easings more confident. Increase entrance durations slightly to signal weight.

What NOT to add

- Glassmorphism, gradient text (`background-clip: text`), drop-shadows on everything, decorative gradients as fill, AI-shimmer haze.
- Side-stripe colored borders on cards.
- More clutter — bolder is restraint with conviction, not maximalism.

Test
After your changes, the design should still feel intentional and clean. If it now feels loud or cluttered, you went too far — pull back on one move.
